Water

Water is at the core of sustainable development and is critical for socio-economic development, energy and food production, healthy ecosystems and for human survival itself. Water is also at the heart of adaptation to climate change, serving as the crucial link between society and the environment.

Water is also a rights issue. As the global population grows, there is an increasing need to balance all of the competing commercial demands on water resources, so that communities have enough for their needs. In particular, women and girls must have access to clean, private sanitation facilities to manage menstruation and maternity in dignity and safety.

What can we do

Close The Tap

Closing the tap after using it may not seem like it will make any diffrence but if we all do it everyday we will start to see the diffrence in no time.

Shorter Showers

Taking a relaxing shower after a long day of work may sound pretty good, but think of those who don't have enough water to drink in Africa. Taking a shorter shower doesn't seem like much but it does make a diffrence.

Check for leaks

Even a small drip can waste 50 or more gallons of water a day. So check your faucets and if there are leaks call someone to fix it. These small actions make a huge diffrence and remember maybe you are the reason a child can drink some
